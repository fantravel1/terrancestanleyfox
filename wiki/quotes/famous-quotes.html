Terry Fox's Famous Quotes

Words That Continue to Inspire Millions

The Power of Terry's Words

Terry Fox was not a polished speaker or a trained orator. He was a 21-year-old with a dream and the courage to pursue it. Yet his words‚Äîsimple, honest, and filled with determination‚Äîhave inspired millions of people around the world for over four decades.

These quotes, captured during the Marathon of Hope and in interviews, reveal the depth of Terry's character and the unwavering focus that drove him to attempt the impossible.

"I'm not a dreamer, and I'm not saying this will initiate any kind of definitive answer or cure to cancer, but I believe in miracles. I have to."

On Determination

"I just wish people would realize that anything's possible if you try; dreams are made possible if you try."
Spoken during a television interview in Ontario, July 1980. This quote captures Terry's core belief that determination trumps natural ability.
"Even if I don't finish, we need others to continue. It's got to keep going without me."
Said during the Marathon of Hope, reflecting Terry's understanding that his mission was bigger than himself. This quote foreshadowed the Terry Fox Run.
"I'm not a quitter."
A simple statement that defined Terry's approach to everything from basketball to his cross-Canada run.

On the Marathon of Hope

"Somewhere the hurting must stop."
From Terry's original letter to the Canadian Cancer Society, explaining why he wanted to run across Canada. He was referring both to his own pain and to the suffering of all cancer patients.
"I want to set an example that will never be forgotten."
Spoken at the start of the Marathon of Hope in St. John's, Newfoundland, April 12, 1980.
"When I started this run, I said that if we all gave one dollar, we'd have $22 million for cancer research, and I don't care man, there's no reason that isn't possible."
Terry's plea to Canadians, asking for just one dollar from each person. By the time the Marathon ended, Canadians had given far more.

On Cancer and Hope

"I had to do something... I couldn't just sit back and watch everyone else raise money while I did nothing. I had to be involved in some way."
Explaining his motivation for the Marathon of Hope, revealing that he couldn't accept a passive role in the fight against cancer.
"Nobody is ever going to call me a quitter."
Said during a difficult day on the Marathon when Terry was struggling with pain and exhaustion.
"How many people do something they really believe in? I just wish people would realize that anything is possible if you try."
Reflecting on his decision to attempt the Marathon despite overwhelming odds against success.

On Perseverance

"I got satisfaction out of doing things that were difficult. It was an incredible feeling."
Describing how he approached challenges, whether in sports or in his fight against cancer.
"It took me 14 months to accept the fact that I was an amputee. In that 14 months, I was sad, depressed, and I really didn't know what I was going to do with my life."
An honest reflection on the difficult period following his amputation, showing that Terry's determination was forged through struggle, not instant acceptance.
"I remember promising myself that should I live, I would rise up to meet the challenge before me."
From Terry's personal writings about his cancer diagnosis and the birth of the Marathon of Hope idea.

His Final Words

"I've got to try. I've got to keep going."
One of Terry's last statements during the Marathon, near Thunder Bay, before he was forced to stop due to the return of his cancer.
"I just wish people would realize that anything's possible if you try; dreams are made possible if you try."

The Legacy of His Words

Terry Fox's quotes have been displayed in schools, hospitals, and community centers across Canada and around the world. They appear on monuments, in books, and on the walls of the Terry Fox Foundation offices.

What makes Terry's words so powerful is their authenticity. He wasn't trying to be inspirational‚Äîhe was simply speaking from the heart about what he believed and what he was trying to accomplish. That honesty resonates with people of all ages and backgrounds.

Today, Terry's quotes continue to inspire millions of people participating in Terry Fox Runs worldwide. His words remind us that ordinary people can achieve extraordinary things when they commit fully to a cause larger than themselves.
