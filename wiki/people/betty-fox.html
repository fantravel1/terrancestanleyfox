Betty Fox

Terry's Mother & Foundation Champion (1935-2024)

Early Life

Betty Chicken was born in Melfort, Saskatchewan, in 1935. She met Rolland Fox in the early 1950s, and they married in 1955. The couple had four children: Fred (1956), Terry (1958), Darrell (1962), and Judith (1965).

The family initially lived in Winnipeg, Manitoba, where Terry was born, before moving to British Columbia in the mid-1960s. Betty was a dedicated homemaker who instilled in her children the values of hard work, determination, and helping others‚Äîvalues that would define Terry's character.

Supporting Terry

When Terry was diagnosed with osteosarcoma in 1976, Betty was by his side throughout his treatment and recovery. She supported his decision to run across Canada, even though she worried constantly about his health.

During the Marathon of Hope, Betty remained at home in Port Coquitlam but stayed in close contact with Terry. She flew to meet him at key points along the route, including his triumphant arrival in Toronto.

After Terry's Death

When Terry passed away on June 28, 1981, Betty channeled her grief into continuing his mission. She became the public face of the Terry Fox Foundation, speaking at events, meeting with researchers, and attending Terry Fox Runs around the world.

For over 40 years, Betty traveled tirelessly, sharing Terry's story with new generations. She visited schools, spoke at fundraising galas, and met with world leaders‚Äîall in service of her son's dream.

Legacy

Betty's dedication helped transform the Terry Fox Run from a Canadian event into a global movement. Under her watchful guidance, the Foundation maintained Terry's principles: no entry fees, no minimum pledge, and 100% of funds going to cancer research.

She received numerous honors for her work, including the Order of Canada and the Order of British Columbia. But Betty always deflected praise, insisting that the recognition belonged to Terry and to the millions of Canadians who participated in his mission.

Final Years

Betty remained active with the Foundation well into her 80s, though she reduced her travel schedule in later years. She passed away peacefully on June 17, 2024, at her home in Port Coquitlam, British Columbia, at the age of 88.

Her passing was mourned across Canada, with tributes from Prime Ministers, celebrities, and ordinary Canadians whose lives had been touched by Terry's story‚Äîa story that Betty had kept alive for over four decades.
