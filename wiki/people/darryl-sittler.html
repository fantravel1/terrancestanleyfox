Darryl Sittler

Overview

Darryl Sittler (born September 18, 1950) is a Canadian former professional ice hockey player who played in the National Hockey League (NHL). As captain of the Toronto Maple Leafs, he was one of the most beloved hockey players of his era.

Sittler is remembered not only for his hockey achievements but also for one of the most memorable moments of the Marathon of Hope: giving Terry Fox his 1980 NHL All-Star jersey at Nathan Phillips Square on July 11, 1980.

Quick Facts

  • Born: September 18, 1950, Kitchener, Ontario
  • Position: Centre
  • NHL Career: 1970-1985
  • Teams: Toronto Maple Leafs, Philadelphia Flyers, Detroit Red Wings
  • Hall of Fame: Inducted 1989
  • Jersey Number: 27 (retired by Maple Leafs)

The Jersey Gift

On July 11, 1980, an estimated 10,000 people gathered at Nathan Phillips Square in Toronto to welcome Terry Fox during the Marathon of Hope. Among the dignitaries on stage was Darryl Sittler, captain of the Toronto Maple Leafs and one of the most famous athletes in Canada.

What happened next became one of the most iconic moments of the Marathon. Sittler approached Terry on stage and, in a spontaneous gesture of admiration, removed his 1980 NHL All-Star jersey and presented it to Terry.

"I've met a lot of brave people in my life. But I've never met anyone as brave as Terry Fox. Terry is the real all-star." ‚Äî Darryl Sittler, July 11, 1980

The Significance

Sittler's gesture was powerful for several reasons. In hockey-obsessed Canada, a player giving away his All-Star jersey was almost unheard of. The jersey represented Sittler's selection as one of the best players in the NHL that year‚Äîand he was saying Terry deserved it more.

For Terry, receiving recognition from a sports hero was deeply meaningful. Terry had grown up playing hockey and basketball, dreaming of athletic glory. Now, despite‚Äîor because of‚Äîlosing his leg, one of Canada's greatest athletes was calling him the "real all-star."

Hockey Career

Sittler's NHL career was legendary in its own right. He holds the record for most points in a single game (10 points on February 7, 1976) and was a ten-time NHL All-Star. He captained the Maple Leafs during some of the team's most memorable years.

His jersey number 27 was retired by the Toronto Maple Leafs, and he was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1989. He remains one of the most beloved figures in Toronto sports history.

Continued Support

After the Marathon of Hope, Sittler remained a supporter of the Terry Fox Foundation. He has participated in Terry Fox Runs and lent his name and presence to cancer research fundraising.

The connection forged that day in Toronto‚Äîbetween a hockey legend and a one-legged runner‚Äîsymbolized how Terry's mission transcended sports. He wasn't just a runner; he was an inspiration to everyone, including Canada's greatest athletes.

"Terry Fox showed all of us what real courage looks like. What he did makes everything I ever did on the ice seem small by comparison." ‚Äî Darryl Sittler, reflecting on Terry's legacy

Where is the Jersey Now?

The NHL All-Star jersey that Sittler gave Terry is now part of the Terry Fox archives, preserved as one of the most significant artifacts from the Marathon of Hope. It represents the moment when Terry's mission captured the heart of the nation.
