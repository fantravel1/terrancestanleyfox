Books About Terry Fox

Essential Reading on a Canadian Hero

Overview

Terry Fox's extraordinary life has been documented in numerous books, ranging from the definitive biography to children's picture books. These works preserve Terry's story for future generations and continue to inspire readers worldwide.

The books listed here represent the most significant published works about Terry, including those authorized by the Fox family and scholarly examinations of his impact on Canadian culture.

Definitive Biography

üìñ Terry Fox: His Story

by Leslie Scrivener
Publisher
McClelland & Stewart
First Published
1981
Updated Edition
2005
Pages
~300

This is THE definitive biography of Terry Fox, authorized by the Fox family and written by journalist Leslie Scrivener. Drawing on extensive interviews with Betty Fox, Doug Alward, and others who knew Terry, Scrivener crafted a detailed and moving account of his life.

The book covers Terry's childhood, his cancer diagnosis, the 18 months of training, and the day-by-day progress of the Marathon of Hope. It remains the primary source for most other works about Terry.

Academic and Cultural Studies

üìñ The Meaning of Terry Fox

Various Contributors
Type
Academic Essays
A collection of essays examining Terry Fox's impact on Canadian identity, disability rights, and charitable giving. Contributors analyze why Terry's story resonates so deeply with Canadians and how his legacy has evolved over time.

Memoirs and Related Works

by Isadore Sharp
Relevance

While primarily about the Four Seasons hotel chain, Isadore Sharp's memoir includes a significant section on his relationship with Terry Fox and the founding of the Terry Fox Run.

Educational Materials

In addition to published books, the Terry Fox Foundation produces educational materials for schools, including:

Teacher's Guides: Curriculum-aligned materials for teaching about Terry Fox at various grade levels.

Activity Books: Worksheets and activities that help students engage with Terry's story.

Video Resources: Documentary clips and educational videos that accompany printed materials.

These materials are available free to Canadian schools through the Terry Fox School Run program.
