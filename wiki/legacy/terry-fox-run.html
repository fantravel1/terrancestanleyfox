The Terry Fox Run

World's Largest One-Day Fundraiser for Cancer Research

Overview

The Terry Fox Run is an annual non-competitive charity event held in support of cancer research. Taking place on the second Sunday after Labour Day each September, it has grown from a Canadian tradition into a global phenomenon, with events held in over 60 countries worldwide.

Since the first run in 1981, participants have raised over $850 million for cancer research, making it the world's largest one-day fundraiser for the cause. The event honors Terry Fox's legacy and continues his mission of finding a cure for cancer.

The First Terry Fox Run

The first Terry Fox Run was held on September 13, 1981, just three months after Terry's death on June 28, 1981. The event was organized in response to Terry's wish that his mission continue even if he couldn't finish the Marathon of Hope himself.

Isadore Sharp, founder of Four Seasons Hotels and a key supporter during the Marathon, was instrumental in organizing the first run. He had promised Terry that if the Marathon couldn't be completed, an annual run would be held in his name.

Terry's Principles

The Terry Fox Run maintains the principles Terry established during the Marathon of Hope. These guidelines ensure that the event remains true to his vision:

Core Principles

  • No Entry Fee: Everyone can participate regardless of ability to pay
  • No Minimum Pledge: Every donation, no matter how small, is valued
  • Non-Competitive: Participants walk, run, cycle, or roll at their own pace
  • Inclusive: Open to people of all ages and abilities
  • 100% to Research: All funds go directly to cancer research

These principles reflect Terry's belief that the fight against cancer belongs to everyone. He wanted to include as many Canadians as possible in his mission, and the Terry Fox Run continues that inclusive spirit.

Global Expansion

While the Terry Fox Run began as a Canadian event, it quickly expanded internationally. Terry's story resonated with people around the world, and communities in other countries wanted to participate in his mission.

The Terry Fox School Run

In addition to the main September event, the Terry Fox School Run brings Terry's message to students across Canada. Held during the last week of September, the School Run is one of the largest student-focused fundraising events in Canada.

Over 9,000 schools participate annually, teaching new generations about Terry's courage and inspiring young people to support cancer research. Many adults who participate in Terry Fox Runs today first learned about Terry through their school run experience.

How the Run Works

Terry Fox Runs are organized locally by volunteers. Each site chooses its own route‚Äîtypically 5 to 10 kilometers‚Äîand participants can walk, run, cycle, rollerblade, or use wheelchairs to complete the course.

There are no timers, no winners, and no prizes for speed. The focus is entirely on participation and fundraising. Many participants wear Terry Fox t-shirts and carry signs honoring loved ones affected by cancer.

Impact on Cancer Research

The funds raised through Terry Fox Runs have transformed cancer research in Canada and around the world. The Terry Fox Foundation funds innovative research projects that might not receive traditional funding.

Research funded by the Foundation has contributed to breakthroughs in understanding cancer genetics, developing targeted therapies, and improving survival rates for various cancers. Terry's dream of a world without cancer continues to drive scientific progress.
