Cancer Research Funding

Over $850 Million Invested in Finding a Cure

Overview

Since the first Terry Fox Run in 1981, the Terry Fox Foundation has funded more than $850 million in cancer research across Canada and around the world. This makes the Foundation one of the largest funders of cancer research in Canadian history.

Every dollar raised goes directly to research‚Äîthe Foundation operates on a model where 100% of donations fund scientific work, with administrative costs covered separately. This commitment to efficiency honors Terry Fox's wish that his Marathon of Hope make a real difference.

The Terry Fox Research Institute

In 2007, the Foundation established the Terry Fox Research Institute (TFRI) to coordinate large-scale, collaborative cancer research across Canada. The Institute brings together researchers from multiple institutions to work on major questions that no single lab could address alone.

TFRI focuses on translational research‚Äîwork that bridges the gap between laboratory discoveries and treatments that help patients. This approach reflects Terry's pragmatic focus on making a real difference in people's lives.

Terry Fox Research Institute Focus Areas

  • Precision Medicine: Tailoring treatments to individual patients
  • Immunotherapy: Using the body's immune system to fight cancer
  • Early Detection: Finding cancer before it spreads
  • Pediatric Cancers: Focusing on cancers that affect children
  • Cancer Stem Cells: Understanding how cancers regenerate

Major Research Breakthroughs

Funding from the Terry Fox Foundation has contributed to numerous advances in cancer research:

Genetic Understanding

Canadian researchers, supported by Terry Fox funding, have made significant contributions to understanding the genetic basis of cancer. This includes identifying genes that drive tumor growth and developing tests to determine which patients will respond to specific treatments.

Immunotherapy Advances

The Foundation has invested heavily in immunotherapy research‚Äîtreatments that harness the body's own immune system to attack cancer cells. Canadian researchers have contributed to breakthroughs in CAR-T cell therapy and checkpoint inhibitors.

Improved Survival Rates

When Terry was diagnosed with osteosarcoma in 1977, the five-year survival rate for bone cancer was approximately 15-20%. Today, thanks to research advances, that rate has improved to 60-80% for many types of bone cancer.

How Funding Works

The Terry Fox Foundation uses a rigorous peer-review process to allocate research funding:

Research Funding Process

  • Step 1: Researchers submit grant applications
  • Step 2: Expert panels review proposals
  • Step 3: Peer review ensures scientific merit
  • Step 4: Board approves successful applications
  • Step 5: Researchers receive multi-year funding
  • Step 6: Annual reports track progress

Training Future Researchers

Beyond funding research projects, the Terry Fox Foundation invests in training the next generation of cancer researchers. Graduate students and postdoctoral fellows receive support to pursue careers in cancer research.

The Terry Fox New Frontiers Program specifically supports early-career researchers with innovative ideas. Many of today's leading cancer researchers received early support from Terry Fox funding.

International Impact

While the Foundation is based in Canada, its research impact extends worldwide. Canadian researchers collaborate with international partners, and discoveries made with Terry Fox funding benefit patients globally.

International Terry Fox Runs contribute to the research fund, creating a global community united in the fight against cancer.

The Path Forward

Terry's dream was a world without cancer. While that goal has not yet been achieved, the research funded in his name has saved countless lives and continues to bring us closer to effective treatments and eventual cures.

The Terry Fox Foundation remains committed to funding innovative, high-impact research that will continue Terry's mission for generations to come.
