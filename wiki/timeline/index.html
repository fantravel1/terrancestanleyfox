Complete Timeline

A comprehensive chronology of Terry Fox's life, Marathon of Hope, and continuing legacy.

Early Life (1958-1976)

July 28, 1958
Terry Fox is Born
Terrance Stanley Fox is born at Grace Hospital in Winnipeg, Manitoba, to Rolland and Betty Fox.
~1966
Family Moves West
The Fox family relocates to British Columbia, eventually settling in Port Coquitlam.
1973-1976
High School Years
Terry attends Port Coquitlam Senior Secondary, playing basketball and developing his legendary determination.
September 1976
Enrolls at SFU
Terry begins studying kinesiology at Simon Fraser University.

Cancer & Recovery (1976-1979)

November 1976
Cancer Diagnosis
Terry is diagnosed with osteosarcoma (bone cancer) in his right leg.
March 9, 1977
Amputation Surgery
Terry's right leg is amputated 15 cm above the knee at Royal Columbian Hospital.
April 1977
First Prosthetic Leg
Just three weeks after surgery, Terry is fitted with his first artificial leg.
1977-1978
Chemotherapy
Terry undergoes 16 months of chemotherapy treatment while secretly training to run.
August 1979
First Marathon
Terry completes the Prince George Labatt Marathon, finishing last but proving his dream is possible.
October 1979
Writes to Cancer Society
Terry sends his famous letter to the Canadian Cancer Society proposing the Marathon of Hope.

Marathon of Hope (1980)

April 12, 1980
Marathon Begins
Terry dips his artificial leg in the Atlantic Ocean at St. John's, Newfoundland, and begins running west.
May 1980
Maritime Provinces
Terry runs through Newfoundland, Nova Scotia, PEI, and New Brunswick with growing public support.
June-July 1980
Quebec & Ontario
Momentum builds as Terry reaches Montreal and major Ontario cities.
July 1, 1980
Canada Day
Terry is celebrated across Canada on Canada Day, becoming a national hero.
July 11, 1980
Toronto
Over 10,000 people greet Terry at Nathan Phillips Square in Toronto.
September 1, 1980
Marathon Ends
After 143 days and 5,373 km, Terry is forced to stop near Thunder Bay when cancer spreads to his lungs.
September 1980
Order of Canada
Terry becomes the youngest Companion of the Order of Canada.

Legacy (1981-Present)

June 28, 1981
Terry Passes Away
Terry Fox dies at Royal Columbian Hospital in New Westminster, BC, one month before his 23rd birthday.
September 13, 1981
First Terry Fox Run
Over 300,000 Canadians participate in the first Terry Fox Run, raising $3.5 million.
1981
Terry Fox Foundation
The Terry Fox Foundation is established to continue funding cancer research.
2004
Greatest Canadian
Terry is voted the Greatest Canadian of all time in a CBC Television poll.
Present
Continuing Legacy
Annual Terry Fox Runs in 60+ countries have raised over $850 million for cancer research.
