Early Life & Family

Explore the formative years that shaped Terrance Stanley Fox - from his birth in Winnipeg to his childhood in Port Coquitlam, and the family that raised a hero.

Overview

Terrance Stanley Fox was born on July 28, 1958, in Winnipeg, Manitoba, to Rolland Fox and Betty Fox (n√©e Chicken). He was the second of four children, growing up with older brother Fred, and younger siblings Darrell and Judith.

When Terry was young, the Fox family moved west to Surrey, British Columbia, and later to Port Coquitlam, where Terry would spend his formative years. The move to British Columbia would prove significant - it was in Port Coquitlam that Terry developed his legendary determination through sports and formed the friendships that would later support his Marathon of Hope.

Family Background

The Fox family was of English and Scottish descent, representing the working-class Canadian values that Terry would later embody in his marathon. Rolland Fox worked as a switchman for the Canadian National Railway, while Betty was a homemaker who later became one of Terry's most vocal advocates and the driving force behind the Terry Fox Foundation.

The family lived modestly but happily, with a strong emphasis on hard work, honesty, and perseverance - values that would become central to Terry's character. Family members later recalled that even as a child, Terry showed remarkable determination and refused to accept defeat in anything he attempted.
