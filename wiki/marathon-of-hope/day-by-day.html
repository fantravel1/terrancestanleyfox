Marathon of Hope: Day by Day

143 Days That Changed Canada Forever

Overview

From April 12 to September 1, 1980, Terry Fox ran across Canada, covering 5,373 kilometers (3,339 miles) in 143 days. He ran the equivalent of a marathon every day‚Äîapproximately 42 kilometers‚Äîon one leg and a prosthetic.

This page chronicles key moments from the Marathon of Hope, tracking Terry's progress from the Atlantic to where the journey ended near Thunder Bay, Ontario.

St. John's, Newfoundland
Terry dips his artificial leg in the Atlantic Ocean at Mile Zero, filling two bottles with seawater‚Äîone to keep, one to pour in the Pacific. The Marathon of Hope begins.
Days 2-28 - April 13 - May 9
Crossing Newfoundland
Terry battles fierce Atlantic weather, including snow, sleet, and cold rain. The roads are difficult and donations are minimal. Isolation and anonymity mark these early days.
Late April
Port aux Basques
Terry reaches the ferry to Nova Scotia, having completed Newfoundland. The mainland awaits, and with it, hope for more support.

Terry runs through the Maritime province. Communities begin organizing welcoming committees. The Canadian Cancer Society chapters are alerted to his approach.
Late May
Quebec Border
Entering Canada's largest province brings new challenges, including the language barrier. But Quebecers embrace Terry warmly.

Terry runs through Quebec's largest city. French-Canadian media gives extensive coverage. Donations surge as Quebec embraces the Marathon of Hope.
June 22, 1980
Ottawa - Meeting the Prime Minister
Terry meets Prime Minister Pierre Trudeau at the National Capital. The meeting generates national news coverage. Terry is now a household name.
Late June
Entering Ontario
Ontario, Canada's most populous province, awaits. The support is unprecedented. Terry runs past cheering crowds daily.

July 1, 1980
Canada Day
Terry spends Canada Day running through Ontario. Celebrations along the route honor both the country and its newest hero.
Day 86 - July 11, 1980
Toronto - Nathan Phillips Square
10,000 people gather at City Hall. Darryl Sittler gives Terry his All-Star jersey. This is the emotional peak of the Marathon. National television broadcasts the celebration.
Mid-Late July
Northern Ontario
Terry runs through less populated areas, but support remains strong. The summer heat is intense. His prosthetic leg causes increasing problems.
üçÇ August 1980 - The Final Stretch
Early August
Approaching Thunder Bay
Terry develops a persistent cough. Pain in his chest increases. He pushes through, running despite the discomfort.
August 28, 1980
The Cough Worsens
Terry's cough becomes severe. He has difficulty breathing. Doug Alward urges him to rest, but Terry continues.
Day 143 - September 1, 1980
Thunder Bay - The End
After running 18 miles that morning, Terry can no longer continue. He tells Doug, "I gotta stop." Hospital X-rays confirm the cancer has returned and spread to his lungs. The Marathon of Hope ends at 5,373 kilometers.

After September 1

Though Terry could no longer run, the Marathon of Hope continued in spirit. Canadians rallied to complete his mission of raising $1 from every Canadian‚Äî$24 million at the time.

By February 1981, the goal was reached. When Terry died on June 28, 1981, over $23 million had been raised. Today, that total exceeds $850 million through the annual Terry Fox Run.
