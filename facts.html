About Terry Fox's Life

When was Terry Fox born?

Terrance Stanley Fox was born on July 28, 1958, in Winnipeg, Manitoba, Canada. He was the second of four children born to Rolland and Betty Fox. His family moved to Surrey, British Columbia, when Terry was young, and later to Port Coquitlam, where he grew up and attended Port Coquitlam Senior Secondary School.

When did Terry Fox die?

Terry Fox died on June 28, 1981, at the age of 22. He passed away at Royal Columbian Hospital in New Westminster, British Columbia, approximately one month before his 23rd birthday. His death was caused by the spread of his osteosarcoma (bone cancer) to his lungs.

How did Terry Fox die?

Terry Fox died from osteosarcoma, a form of bone cancer that had spread to his lungs. He was originally diagnosed with osteosarcoma in his right leg in 1977, which resulted in the amputation of his leg 6 inches above the knee. During his Marathon of Hope in 1980, the cancer returned and spread to his lungs. Despite aggressive treatment including chemotherapy, the cancer proved too advanced.

Where did Terry Fox die?

Terry Fox died at Royal Columbian Hospital in New Westminster, British Columbia, Canada. After being forced to stop his Marathon of Hope in Thunder Bay, Ontario, he returned to British Columbia for cancer treatment. His funeral was held at Christ Church in Port Coquitlam, and he is buried at Port Coquitlam Cemetery.

Where did Terry Fox live?

Terry Fox was born in Winnipeg, Manitoba, but grew up primarily in British Columbia. His family moved to Surrey, BC, when he was young, and later to Port Coquitlam, BC, where he spent most of his life. He attended Port Coquitlam Senior Secondary School and Simon Fraser University, where he played basketball.

How did Terry Fox lose his leg?

Terry Fox lost his right leg to osteosarcoma (bone cancer) in 1977 when he was 18 years old. He first noticed pain in his knee while playing basketball. After the pain persisted, he was diagnosed with osteogenic sarcoma. His right leg was amputated 6 inches (15 cm) above the knee on March 9, 1977, at Royal Columbian Hospital.

The Marathon of Hope

When did Terry Fox start his run?

Terry Fox started his Marathon of Hope on April 12, 1980. He dipped his artificial leg in the Atlantic Ocean at St. John's, Newfoundland, and began running west across Canada. His goal was to run the entire country from coast to coast. He planned to dip his leg in the Pacific Ocean upon completion.

Where did Terry Fox end his run?

Terry Fox was forced to end his Marathon of Hope just outside Thunder Bay, Ontario, on September 1, 1980, after running for 143 days and covering 5,373 kilometers. He stopped when the cancer spread to his lungs. This location is now marked by the Terry Fox Memorial and Lookout, a nine-foot bronze statue overlooking Lake Superior.

When did Terry Fox stop running?

Terry Fox stopped running on September 1, 1980, just outside Thunder Bay, Ontario. After 143 days and 5,373 kilometers, he was forced to stop when the cancer spread to his lungs. That morning, Terry felt chest pains and difficulty breathing. He asked his friend Doug Alward to drive him to a hospital, where doctors discovered the cancer had returned.

Where did Terry Fox stop running?

Terry Fox stopped running on the Trans-Canada Highway just east of Thunder Bay, Ontario, near mile marker 3,339. The exact location is now commemorated by the Terry Fox Memorial and Lookout, featuring a nine-foot bronze statue. The Terry Fox Courage Highway (Highway 17 from Thunder Bay to Nipigon) was also named in his honor.

How long did Terry Fox run?

Terry Fox ran for 143 consecutive days during his Marathon of Hope, from April 12 to September 1, 1980. During this time, he covered 5,373 kilometers (3,339 miles) across Canada, running the equivalent of a full marathon (42 km) every single day through six Canadian provinces.

How much did Terry Fox run?

Terry Fox ran a total of 5,373 kilometers (3,339 miles) during his Marathon of Hope. He averaged approximately 42 kilometers (26 miles) per day - the equivalent of a full marathon - for 143 consecutive days. This incredible feat was accomplished on one leg and a prosthetic limb.

Where did Terry Fox run?

Terry Fox ran across Eastern and Central Canada. His route started in St. John's, Newfoundland, and went through Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island, New Brunswick, Quebec, and Ontario. Key cities included Halifax, Charlottetown, Moncton, Montreal, Ottawa, and Toronto. He stopped near Thunder Bay, Ontario.

When did Terry Fox run?

Terry Fox ran his Marathon of Hope in 1980, from April 12 to September 1. He ran through spring, summer, and into early fall, facing various weather conditions including rain, heat, wind, and cold. Each day he ran approximately 42 kilometers regardless of weather or physical condition.

When did Terry Fox run across Canada?

Terry Fox ran across Canada in 1980 during his Marathon of Hope. He started on April 12, 1980, in St. John's, Newfoundland, and ran westward for 143 days until September 1, 1980. While he did not complete his coast-to-coast journey, his run across Eastern Canada galvanized the nation.

When did Terry Fox end his run?

Terry Fox ended his Marathon of Hope on September 1, 1980, near Thunder Bay, Ontario. After experiencing chest pains and coughing fits that morning, he was taken to hospital where X-rays revealed the cancer had spread to his lungs. In a tearful press conference, Terry told Canadians about his condition.

His Purpose & Legacy

Why did Terry Fox run?

Terry Fox ran to raise money and awareness for cancer research after being diagnosed with bone cancer and losing his right leg. While in hospital, he was deeply affected by the suffering of other cancer patients, especially children. He was inspired by amputee Dick Traum who completed the New York City Marathon. Terry wanted to give hope to cancer patients and fund research to find a cure.

What was Terry Fox's goal?

Terry Fox's goal was twofold: to run across Canada from coast to coast, and to raise $1 from every Canadian for cancer research. With Canada's population at approximately 24 million, this meant raising $24 million. He exceeded this goal, raising $24.17 million during his lifetime. His broader goal was to increase awareness about cancer research and give hope to others.

How much did Terry Fox raise?

During his lifetime, Terry Fox's Marathon of Hope raised $24.17 million for cancer research. Since then, the annual Terry Fox Run has raised over $850 million worldwide. The Terry Fox Foundation continues to fund innovative cancer research, making it one of the most successful fundraising efforts in history.

How much has Terry Fox raised?

The Terry Fox Foundation has raised over $850 million for cancer research since 1980. The annual Terry Fox Run, held in over 60 countries, raises approximately $20-30 million each year. All funds go directly to cancer research with no athlete fees or prize money, making it one of the largest single-day fundraisers for cancer research in the world.

What did Terry Fox do for Canada?

Terry Fox united Canadians around a common cause and became a symbol of courage, determination, and hope. He raised unprecedented awareness and funding for cancer research. He inspired the annual Terry Fox Run, now held in over 60 countries. Terry was named a Companion of the Order of Canada and has had numerous schools and buildings named after him.

Why is Terry Fox a Canadian national hero?

Terry Fox is a Canadian national hero because he embodied courage, determination, selflessness, and perseverance. Despite losing his leg to cancer, he attempted to run across Canada. His Marathon of Hope united the nation and inspired millions worldwide. He was voted the greatest Canadian of all time in a 2004 CBC poll. His legacy continues through the annual Terry Fox Run and over $850 million raised for cancer research.

Why is Terry Fox a hero?

Terry Fox is a hero because of his extraordinary courage, selflessness, and determination. Despite losing his leg to cancer at 18, he ran across Canada to raise money for cancer research. He ran a marathon a day for 143 days on one leg. His goal wasn't personal glory but helping find a cure for cancer. His famous quote, "Anything is possible if you try," continues to inspire millions.

What is Terry Fox known for?

Terry Fox is known for his Marathon of Hope, an attempted cross-Canada run to raise money for cancer research. After losing his right leg to bone cancer at age 18, Terry ran 5,373 kilometers over 143 days on one leg and a prosthetic. His quote "Anything is possible if you try" remains iconic. He's recognized as one of the greatest Canadians ever.

Terry Fox Day & Annual Run

What is Terry Fox Day?

Terry Fox Day is a commemorative day honoring Terry Fox and his Marathon of Hope. In Manitoba, it is a statutory holiday observed on the first Monday of August. Other provinces recognize it differently, often coinciding with the annual Terry Fox Run in September. The day celebrates Terry's courage and promotes ongoing cancer research.

When is Terry Fox Day in Ontario?

In Ontario, Terry Fox Day is celebrated on the second Sunday after Labour Day, typically falling in mid-September. This coincides with the annual Terry Fox Run events. The day honors Terry's memory and the continuing mission to raise funds for cancer research.

When is Terry Fox Day in Canada?

Terry Fox Day is observed differently across Canada. In Manitoba, it is a statutory holiday on the first Monday of August. In Ontario and other provinces, it is typically recognized on the second Sunday after Labour Day in September, coinciding with the annual Terry Fox Run.

When is the Terry Fox Run 2025?

The Terry Fox Run 2025 is scheduled for Sunday, September 14, 2025. The annual run is held on the second Sunday after Labour Day across Canada and in communities around the world. Millions of participants will walk, run, bike, or roll to raise funds for cancer research. Visit terryfox.org for details.
